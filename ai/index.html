About

I'm a 21-year-old AI student at Utrecht University, driven by a genuine curiosity about how intelligent systems think — and where they go wrong. My academic path started in Applied Physics at TU Delft before I pivoted to Artificial Intelligence, bringing an analytical, first-principles mindset to everything I do.

My current research focuses on social sycophancy in LLMs — studying how large language models adjust their responses based on perceived social pressure, both in UI and API environments. It sits at the intersection of NLP, HCI, and AI safety, which is exactly where I want to be.

Outside the lab I boulder, draw, and make music — all disciplines that reward deliberate practice and pattern recognition, not unlike machine learning itself.
