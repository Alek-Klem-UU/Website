4.Current Research

The author's BSc thesis investigates social sycophancy in large language models — the phenomenon whereby model outputs shift in response to social pressure cues embedded in user prompts, independent of factual merit. This behaviour has significant implications for AI safety, as sycophantic models may reinforce incorrect beliefs and reduce the reliability of AI-assisted decision-making.

The methodology involves a fully automated web-interaction pipeline capable of submitting structured prompts to both conversational UI environments and direct API endpoints, enabling systematic comparison of model behaviour across interaction modalities. See github.com/Alek-Klem-UU/SycoUI.
